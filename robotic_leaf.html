Reprogramming the Dipendenza: A New Interdisciplinary Roofscape"

A renovation at the Swiss Institute in Rome is planned over the next few years. Currently the Swiss institute splits its program across its two buildings. At the moment they provide both residential and work related functions. This often creates disparities between its users, resulting in what we believe to be a dysfunctional program. Our aim is to designate the villa as a mainly residential building while relocating every work related function to the dipendenza. To do this we plan to reuse the interior spaces to provide labs, storage, offices and ateliers instead of the former apartments. The inner program of the dipendenza is complemented by an extension on the last floor. It consists of an open space under a new roof that results in a representative space for the institute that is well connected to the topography of the site. We intend to create a workspace that is meant to favour interdisciplinary and interaction between guests on both the artistic and scientific front. This new roof construction follows the typology of a mediterranean roof with an interior courtyard. The leaf-like shape (Foglia) is designed to provide a regular reaction to the different orientations of the site. The shape adapts to the corners of the dipendenza while creating a traversing terrace that links both sides of the building to the park, creating an additional passage thanks to the addition of a new outer staircase.

During this experimental semester we learnt the use of parametric software such as Grasshopper and Rhinoceros, we did not develop sections and plans but rather a complete 3D model to extract all the required information to represent the project in the best possible way, right down to the construction details developed in collaboration with BUK to make the project as pragmatic as possible.
